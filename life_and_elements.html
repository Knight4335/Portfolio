Elements in Earth’s Crust and Living Systems

The same elements that make up Earth’s crust are also essential for life processes. These elements move from rocks and soils into plants, animals, and humans through cycles like the carbon cycle, water cycle, and nutrient cycles.

Element Symbol Found in Earth’s Crust as... Role in Living Systems Example in the Body / Nature
Oxygen O Oxides, silicates, water Essential for respiration; part of water and organic molecules Found in every cell; 65% of human body mass
Silicon Si Quartz, sand, silicates Strengthens plant stems and shells Present in grasses and diatom shells
Aluminum Al Feldspar, bauxite Not biologically essential, but sometimes found in small traces Structural in some plant tissues (minor role)
Iron Fe Hematite, magnetite Carries oxygen in blood and helps enzyme function Hemoglobin in red blood cells
Calcium Ca Limestone, gypsum Builds bones, teeth, and shells; aids muscle function Found in bones, coral, and eggshells
Sodium Na Halite (salt), feldspar Maintains nerve impulses and fluid balance Present in blood plasma
Potassium K Feldspar, mica Regulates heartbeat and nerve signals Found inside cells and in fruits like bananas
Magnesium Mg Olivine, dolomite Core atom in chlorophyll for photosynthesis In green plants and human enzymes
Phosphorus P Apatite minerals Forms DNA, RNA, and ATP (energy molecule) Found in bones, teeth, and cells

Key Idea:

Life depends directly on Earth’s materials. The elements that form our rocks and soils are recycled and reused by living organisms, showing the deep connection between geology and biology.
